### Phase 7: Dynamic Risk Tolerance — COMPLETE (core)
- [x] **`risk/dynamic_risk.py` (NEW)**: `DynamicRiskController` + `RiskSnapshot` + `DrawdownTier`. Composes four independent factors into one runtime snapshot:
    1. **Conviction ladder** via `conviction_size_multiplier`: 0.5 (0.50-0.60), 1.0 (0.60-0.75), 1.5 (0.75-0.85), 2.0 (>=0.85). Conviction <0.50 returns 0.0 (no trade).
    2. **Streak scale** via `compute_streak_scale`: reads the last 10 closed trades; >=60% win rate → +0.5x, <=35% → -0.5x, clamped to [0.5, 2.0]. Requires >=5 samples before kicking in.
    3. **Regime scale**: strong_bull +0.10 and +2 positions, bear -0.10 and -1, strong_bear -0.30 and -2 AND blocks new longs entirely.

High-conviction (>=0.90) additionally unlocks a 50% concentration cap (`HIGH_CONVICTION_POSITION_PCT`). `snapshot_for_signal` is pure — does NOT touch the DB; callers must `refresh_streak()` + `refresh_drawdown()` once per cycle.

### Phase 8: Smart PDT Management — COMPLETE (core)
- [x] **`risk/smart_pdt.py` (NEW)**: `SmartPDTPlanner` + `PDTPlan` dataclass + pure helpers. Replaces the static 0.80 day-trade floor with a dynamic threshold composed from three inputs:
    1. **Day-of-week bump** (`_DOW_BUMP`): Mon +0.05, Tue +0.03, Wed +0.02, Thu 0, Fri -0.05. Early-week stinginess because the full week is ahead; late-week looseness because unused slots expire over the weekend.
    2. **Slots-remaining bump** (`_SLOT_BUMP`): 3 slots -0.02, 2 slots 0, 1 slot +0.03. Last slot raises the bar; fresh budget lets the bot be a bit bolder.

## Architecture Decisions Made
- **Day-to-swing rescue instead of dropping rejected DAY signals**: The brief calls out "Day-to-swing conversion — when PDT is tight, automatically convert DAY/ADAPTIVE signals to SWING". Rather than build a separate conversion path in the strategies, the aggregator checks swing-compatibility via a module-level whitelist and mutates the signal in-place. Rationale: (1) strategies don't need to know about PDT pressure at all — they emit their best signal and the aggregator decides how to spend it, (2) the R:R preservation (1.5x stop + 1.5x target) keeps the original sizing math valid, (3) the `pdt_conversion` metadata breadcrumb gives the trade journal a full audit trail for why a DAY signal became a SWING trade — 2026-04-14
- **Day-of-week + slots-remaining threshold bumps live in module constants, not config**: `_DOW_BUMP` and `_SLOT_BUMP` are hardcoded tables in `smart_pdt.py`. Rationale: (1) these are not frequently-tuned parameters — the intuition ("Monday is stingy, Friday is loose") is stable across account sizes, (2) exposing them as config knobs invites bikeshedding without data backing the tweaks, (3) if a future session proves the tables need adjustment, the Phase 6 parameter optimizer can be extended to propose bumps via `parameter_history` rows — 2026-04-14
- **Intraday strategies (ORB, VWAP) are never day-to-swing converted**: `_SWING_COMPATIBLE_STRATEGIES` deliberately omits `orb` and `vwap`. Rationale: these strategies bet on intraday price action (opening range breakout, VWAP reclaim) — the thesis evaporates at the bell. A converted ORB trade would be holding overnight risk on a setup that was only valid for 30 minutes. The whitelist is a correctness guarantee, not a performance optimization — 2026-04-14
- **Planner is a per-cycle snapshot, not a long-lived stateful controller**: `SmartPDTPlanner.plan_cycle` is called once at the top of `collect_and_rank` and the returned `PDTPlan` is read-only for the rest of the cycle. Rationale: (1) same pattern as the Phase 7 `DynamicRiskController.snapshot_for_signal` — deterministic within a cycle, refreshable between cycles, (2) no hidden state means the logger, the decision audit, and the operator all see the same threshold, (3) testing is trivial — no mocks for cached EV or streaks — 2026-04-14
- **Dynamic risk factors compose in ONE place (`DynamicRiskController`)**: Conviction, streak, regime, and drawdown were all described as independent knobs in the brief. Rather than bolting each one onto the position sizer or the risk manager separately, they multiply into a single `RiskSnapshot` computed per signal. Rationale: (1) the rest of the system stays thin — sizer takes a `risk_scale`, risk manager takes a `max_positions_override`, neither needs to understand WHY, (2) bugs in one factor can't leak into the others because the composition is explicit, (3) future factors (Phase 8 smart PDT, Phase 10 news signals) plug in the same way — 2026-04-14
- **`total_risk_scale` clamped to [0, 3.0]**: Even though the individual factors have bounded ranges, a pathological combination (2.0x conviction × 1.5x streak × 1.1x strong_bull × 1.0x drawdown = 3.3x) would exceed what the risk budget should ever spend. The clamp is a defense-in-depth against future factor additions that might push the product past the ceiling — 2026-04-14
- **Drawdown breaker runs BEFORE the hard daily-loss cap**: `approve_trade` check order is daily_loss → drawdown_breaker → concentration → heat → affordability, but the tiered drawdown in `classify_drawdown` fires at 3% (tier 1, half-size) which is stricter than the 5% daily cap. Rationale: we want graduated de-risking BEFORE hitting the hard kill switch, and the check must run independently of the daily cap because the tier-1 half-size scale is only useful if the trade is still allowed through. `check_daily_loss_limit` stays as a final backstop for configurations where `daily_loss_limit_pct` is higher than the tier-2 threshold — 2026-04-14
- **Streak + drawdown refreshed ONCE per cycle, not per signal**: `SignalAggregator.collect_and_rank` calls `refresh_streak()` and `refresh_drawdown(account_equity)` at the top, then `snapshot_for_signal` is pure. Rationale: DB hits are ~10ms each; a 50-candidate cycle with per-signal refresh would spend 1 second just re-reading the same closed trades. The once-per-cycle pattern matches how the strategy weighter already works — 2026-04-14
- **Controller state persists to `bot_state`, not its own table**: Streak scale, sample size, drawdown tier, and today's starting equity all live under `risk.*` keys in the generic `bot_state` table from Phase 4. Rationale: (1) avoids a new schema migration, (2) the Phase 4 design explicitly covers this kind of ephemeral controller state, (3) one query to inspect all runtime risk state instead of joining multiple tables — 2026-04-14
- **High-conviction concentration cap (50%) is an unlock, not a replacement**: `snapshot_for_signal` sets `max_position_pct = max(base, HIGH_CONVICTION_POSITION_PCT)` only when conviction >= 0.90. Rationale: users who configure a higher base cap (e.g. 0.60 for aggressive accounts) should NOT have it silently lowered to 0.50 for high-conviction signals — the high-conviction lane is a floor not a ceiling — 2026-04-14
- **Parameter optimizer writes via Phase 4 override path, never hot-swaps**: The nightly optimizer writes new `parameter_overrides` rows; the next bot restart picks them up via `apply_parameter_overrides`. Rationale: (1) consistency with the ML predictor's restart-swap design, (2) reproducible within-session behavior so `trade_analysis` rows can be trusted against the parameters that were active at entry, (3) every change is an audit row first (`parameter_history`) and an active override second — the two-phase write makes rollback trivial — 2026-04-14
- **Optimizer is opt-in via `analysis.apply_parameter_changes`**: First sessions will log proposals without mutating strategy parameters. Rationale: this buys observation time to validate the optimizer's judgement on real data before giving it the wheel. `parameter_history` rows are written regardless, so even dry-run sessions produce a training signal — 2026-04-14
- **Bounded ±0.10 adjustment step, hard clamp [0.75, 3.0]**: The brief explicitly calls out "small and incremental — not wild swings". A 0.10 step is ~7% of the 1.5 default, small enough to avoid overshoot but large enough to measurably affect stop quality on the next batch. Hard bounds prevent runaway widening from an edge case — 2026-04-14
- **Loss-cluster scanner uses baseline-delta, not absolute loss rate**: A cluster is flagged when its loss rate exceeds the population baseline by >=20 percentage points. Rationale: absolute thresholds would misfire in bear markets (everything loses) or bull markets (nothing loses). Delta-from-baseline tells you where the strategy is disproportionately broken, regardless of the overall tape — 2026-04-14
- **Breakeven is disabled entirely at conviction >=0.95, not just widened**: The trail ladder caps out at chandelier-only 4x ATR for top-tier signals. Rationale: the breakeven ratchet's purpose is "lock in any profit above entry" — it's an emergency brake, not a trailing strategy. At 95%+ conviction the signal has earned the right to a real retracement instead of being force-closed at entry+0.1% the moment it touches +1 ATR. The IONZ case proved that a hair-trigger breakeven on a strong signal actively converts winners into breakevens — 2026-04-15
- **ATR fallback uses `entry * 0.02`, not `entry - current_stop`**: When a trade row is missing persisted ATR, the old fallback used the stop distance as the ATR proxy. This was circular — strategies that set tight stops (VWAP reclaim near the level) would get artificially small ATR, which would trigger the breakeven ratchet at artificially low price moves, which would ratchet the stop tighter, which would make the next fallback even smaller. 2% of entry price is a conservative volatility floor that doesn't feed back into the stop it's protecting — 2026-04-15
- **`trail_too_tight` is a distinct stop-quality class, not a widened `too_tight`**: The existing `too_tight` check compared reversal_gain to initial_risk, but after trailing, `initial_risk = entry - trailed_stop` collapses to near-zero and the math becomes noise. The new `trail_too_tight` check looks at whether the trade earned >=1/3 of its target before the trailed stop killed it, which is a direct measure of "we surrendered a working thesis". Keeping it separate from `too_tight` preserves the existing classifier's meaning (initial stop placement) while adding a new axis (trail aggressiveness) — 2026-04-15
- **Notification emails are multipart HTML with plaintext fallback, not HTML-only**: `MIMEMultipart("alternative")` attaches both parts; clients pick whichever they can render. Rationale: (1) Gmail web/mobile renders the styled card beautifully, (2) ancient clients and text-only readers still get a readable table, (3) Gmail's preview pane strips HTML anyway so the plaintext version doubles as the preview — 2026-04-15
- **Log timestamps are America/New_York, not UTC or local**: Hard-coding ET matches the market clock regardless of where the bot runs (home, VPS, DigitalOcean US-East). Rationale: (1) the operator's mental model is always ET when watching trades, so the logs should match, (2) UTC required the operator to do +4h/+5h math in their head against DST, (3) machine-local would drift if the bot moves hosts. The millisecond-precision ISO format with offset suffix (`-04:00` / `-05:00`) keeps each line unambiguous even across DST transitions — 2026-04-15
- **State persistence ahead of ML Core**: V2 brief priority list (Section "Priority Order") puts State Persistence (Section 13) before ML Core (Section 12). Prior progress doc had them swapped. Rationale: the ML Core work in Phase 5 will itself need restart-safe model versioning + feature cursor persistence, so shipping the generic `bot_state` / `parameter_overrides` / `strategy_weights` plumbing first means Phase 5 inherits it instead of re-inventing it — 2026-04-14
- **Parameter overrides applied in `__init__`, not at each strategy evaluation**: `apply_parameter_overrides()` patches the `SimpleNamespace` config tree in place before `TradingBot` instantiates strategy objects. Rationale: (1) strategies copy values from config into `self.<param>` in their own `__init__`, so late-binding overrides would require plumbing a "refresh" path through every strategy, (2) in-place config patching is O(overrides), not O(strategies × params), (3) the optimizer (Phase 9) will write a new override row and simply restart to apply it — a clean checkpoint instead of hot-swapping — 2026-04-14
- **Override values stored as TEXT, coerced on load**: The `parameter_overrides.value` column is TEXT and `_coerce()` tries int → float → bool → str on load. Rationale: SQLite mixed-type storage is possible but ugly; TEXT + coerce keeps the schema simple, survives schema migrations without data loss, and matches how `bot_state` works — one mental model for both tables — 2026-04-14
- **Unknown strategies/params in parameter_overrides skip with a warning**: Instead of raising on a stale row (e.g., a strategy that was removed from settings.yaml), the loader logs `parameter_override_skipped_unknown_*` and continues. Rationale: overrides outlive code changes; a config cleanup should never brick startup — 2026-04-14
- **Offline gap detection uses business days, not calendar days**: `detect_offline_gap()` walks day-by-day from last shutdown to now, counting only weekdays. Rationale: a Friday→Monday gap is normal operation (markets are closed), not a 3-day outage. The `flag_for_review` threshold (>5 business days) is the brief's recommendation — 2026-04-14
- **Per-regime parameter overrides via composite PK, not a separate table**: Extended `parameter_overrides` with a `regime` column in the PK rather than creating a `regime_overrides` table. Rationale: (1) one table to query/upsert/delete, (2) `regime=''` naturally represents "global / all regimes" without a NULL-handling footgun, (3) the `get_effective_overrides` method handles the regime+global fallback in a single ordered query with Python dedup — simpler than a UNION or subquery — 2026-04-15
- **Rolling window (last N trades) instead of all-time for optimizer**: The Phase 6 optimizer used all closed trades. Phase 9 defaults to `window=50`. Rationale: market regimes shift; a strategy that needed wide stops 6 months ago in a volatile tape may need tighter stops in a low-vol regime. All-time averaging dilutes the recent signal. The window is configurable per call so the EOD job can tune it — 2026-04-15
- **ParamSpec registry is data, not code**: Each tunable parameter is a frozen dataclass entry in `PARAM_SPECS`, not a custom function. Rationale: (1) adding a new tunable parameter is one dict entry, not a new analysis function, (2) the optimizer loop is parameter-agnostic — it reads `quality_field`, `widen_values`, `tighten_values` generically, (3) the spec's bounds and step are co-located with the signal definition so they can't drift — 2026-04-15
- **Regime persisted to bot_state on every classification, not just EOD**: `record_current_regime` fires inside `_analyze_market_regime` (market open). Rationale: (1) a mid-day restart resumes with the correct regime without waiting for the next classification, (2) `apply_parameter_overrides` at boot can use the regime to load the right overrides immediately, (3) the cost is one `bot_state` upsert per day which is negligible — 2026-04-15
- **Global fallback for missing regime-specific overrides**: `get_effective_overrides(regime)` returns regime-specific rows where available, falling back to `regime=''` rows for (strategy, param) pairs that don't have a regime-specific override. Rationale: operators and the optimizer shouldn't need to populate every regime × strategy × param combination. A global baseline covers the common case; regime-specific overrides are surgical refinements — 2026-04-15
- **ThreadPoolExecutor(3) over asyncio for parallel fetching**: The brief suggests asyncio or ThreadPoolExecutor. Chose ThreadPoolExecutor because: (1) the Alpaca SDK's REST client is synchronous (requests-based), so asyncio would require wrapping every call in `run_in_executor` anyway — no actual concurrency benefit, (2) ThreadPoolExecutor is simpler to wire into the existing synchronous `_evaluate_and_trade` pipeline — no need to convert the entire call chain to async, (3) 3 workers is sufficient (daily bars, intraday bars, news) and bounded — no risk of thread explosion, (4) each future has an independent 30s timeout and error handler so a slow news API doesn't block bar fetching — 2026-04-17
- **WAL mode set per-connection, not once at schema init**: `PRAGMA journal_mode=WAL` executes on every `_conn()` call rather than once in `_init_schema`. Rationale: (1) WAL mode is persistent once set — subsequent PRAGMAs are no-ops (0.01ms overhead), (2) if the DB file is replaced or restored from backup, the next connection still gets WAL without requiring a restart or manual intervention, (3) avoids a special-case "first-run-only" code path — 2026-04-17
- **Cleanup retains core tables indefinitely**: `cleanup_old_data` purges `decisions`, `scanner_results`, `ml_features`, `ml_predictions` but NOT `trades`, `trade_analysis`, `parameter_history`, `strategy_weights`, or `bot_state`. Rationale: (1) trades and analysis are the ML training signal — deleting old trades would degrade model quality, (2) parameter_history is the audit trail for optimizer changes — losing it makes rollback investigation impossible, (3) the high-volume tables (decisions can accumulate 500+ rows/day, scanner_results 50+/day) are the actual DB bloat risk, (4) 90-day retention keeps enough data for the rolling-window optimizer while preventing unbounded growth — 2026-04-17
- **Cycle timer is a CycleTimer instance, not decorators on methods**: The brief suggests "timing decorators to each phase". Chose a context-manager approach (`with timer.phase("fetch")`) over `@timed_phase` decorators because: (1) the phases don't map cleanly to method boundaries — "fetch" spans parallel futures across three data sources, not a single method call, (2) context managers compose with try/except blocks naturally, (3) the `timed_phase` decorator is still available for future method-level instrumentation but the primary timing uses context managers — 2026-04-17
- **Structured event classification over LLM-powered analysis (Phase 11)**: The brief calls for LLM-powered news interpretation, but Phase 11 uses structured keyword categories instead. Rationale: (1) LLM calls cost money and add latency — batch+cache still means $0.01-0.10 per article at 10 articles/symbol/cycle, (2) keyword categories are deterministic and debuggable — you can trace exactly why "rate cut" produced a bullish real_estate impact, (3) the event classifier's output is sufficient for the primary use cases (earnings guard, macro event awareness, sector impact), (4) LLM interpretation can layer on top in a future phase as a "second opinion" on catalyst-detected articles without replacing the baseline. The 60/40 blend (keyword/classifier) in the conviction modifier is designed to shift toward classifier as it proves reliable — 2026-04-16
- **Earnings guard uses news heuristics, not a paid calendar API**: The earnings guard detects upcoming/just-reported earnings by regex-matching article headlines ("to report Q2 earnings", "beats quarterly earnings"). Rationale: (1) Alpaca's news feed reliably publishes earnings-related articles 1-3 days before the event — the heuristic has high recall at zero cost, (2) a paid earnings calendar API ($20-100/month) adds a dependency and failure mode for marginal precision improvement, (3) the guard's false negative rate (missing an upcoming earnings) is low-impact because the news sentiment modifier would already flag the pre-earnings volatility articles as catalyst events — 2026-04-16
- **Economic calendar hardcoded, not fetched from an API**: FOMC/CPI/NFP dates for 2026 are hardcoded module constants. Rationale: (1) the Fed publishes meeting dates a year in advance, CPI/NFP follow predictable monthly cadence, (2) no API dependency means no failure mode on event days when reliability matters most, (3) updating the dates annually is a 5-minute task when the Fed publishes the next year's schedule, (4) the hardcoded approach is the same pattern used by professional quant shops that embed the calendar in their codebase — 2026-04-16
- **Conviction modifiers for economic events are multiplicative, not additive**: FOMC day applies 0.85x, CPI 0.85x. If both fall on the same day (unlikely but possible), the modifier is 0.85 * 0.85 = 0.72. Rationale: (1) multiplicative stacking naturally caps the total impact (can never go below 0), (2) two HIGH events on the same day SHOULD produce a stronger reduction than either alone — the uncertainty compounds, (3) additive would risk negative modifiers at extreme stacking — 2026-04-16
- **News cache TTL is 15 minutes (900s), matching the scan interval**: The news scanner caches results per symbol for 15 minutes. Rationale: (1) scan cycles run every 15 minutes, so the same symbol shouldn't hit the Alpaca API twice in the same cycle, (2) news articles older than 15 minutes are unlikely to have changed — Alpaca publishes, it doesn't update, (3) the cache is in-memory (dict), not persistent — a restart clears it, which is correct since the scan should re-fetch fresh data after any downtime — 2026-04-16
- **Blended 60/40 keyword/classifier conviction modifier**: The news scanner computes both a legacy keyword modifier and a structured event-classifier modifier, then blends 60% keyword + 40% classifier. Rationale: (1) the keyword scorer is battle-tested through Phases 1-10 — replacing it entirely with a new system would be reckless, (2) the classifier adds structured understanding (category weights, sector impacts) that keywords miss, (3) the blend can shift toward 50/50 or 40/60 as the classifier proves reliable in live trading, (4) both systems are deterministic so the blend is reproducible and auditable — 2026-04-16
- **0DTE uses 1.0x ATR target, not 1.5x**: Momentum options targets `price + 1.5 * ATR`; 0DTE uses `1.0 * ATR`. Rationale: 0DTE positions have hours, not days. Theta burns the premium to zero by close, so the move needs to be realistically achievable within the session. 1.0x ATR is a ~68% probability move on a single day, while 1.5x would be a ~50% probability — too aggressive for a position that dies at 4 PM — 2026-04-15

Rationale: (1) these windows are structural — they correspond to the opening momentum fade and the power-hour acceleration, both well-documented market microstructure effects, (2) making them configurable invites tinkering without evidence, (3) the smoke test mocks `_in_entry_window` so testing is unaffected — 2026-04-15

Rationale: (1) 0DTE positions are ephemeral (hours, not days) so a dedicated table adds schema burden for data that's deleted the same day, (2) bot_state already supports the key-value pattern and survives restarts, (3) cleanup happens in the same monitor job that closes the position — no orphaned rows — 2026-04-15

Rationale: (1) 0DTE gamma means prices move fast — 5 minutes could miss a doubling or a wipeout, (2) 1 minute would double the API calls for marginal benefit since Alpaca snapshot data updates ~every 15 seconds anyway, (3) the monitor is O(open_0dte_positions) which is typically 0-2, so the cost per tick is trivial — 2026-04-15

Rationale: 0DTE options on illiquid underlyings have bid-ask spreads that eat 30-50% of premium at entry. No amount of conviction can overcome structural liquidity disadvantage — it's a market constraint, not a signal quality axis — 2026-04-15
- **Central ExitPlanner over per-strategy logic**: Built one shared `strategy/exit_planner.py` that all strategies call via a `_plan_long_exit()` helper on `BaseStrategy`. Rationale: (1) consistent S/R + VIX + regime handling across strategies, (2) one place for Phase 4 ML to swap in model-driven exits without touching strategy code, (3) easy to A/B test planner variants — 2026-04-13
- **Stops only tighten, never loosen**: Both `plan_long_exit` and `compute_trailing_stop_long` reject any proposal that would widen an existing stop. Rationale: the original ATR stop represents the strategy's risk appetite at entry; loosening would break the position sizer's risk-per-share assumption — 2026-04-13

Rationale: the strategy's thesis is "ema_50 is support"; if ema_50 breaks, the trade is invalidated, so that's the correct invalidation level, not a swing low — 2026-04-13

## Known Issues / Tech Debt
- Scanner min_gap_pct=1.5 may starve mean_reversion scan (wants gap DOWN <= -1%)
- ADR uses placeholder 10.0 in screener — not real data
- Covered call/covered straddle require owning 100 shares
- VWAP and ORB depend on intraday_bars fetch in TradingBot
- Strategy weighter burn_in at 5 trades may cause unstable weights early
- Rejections only fire for the **first** failing filter (subsequent filters never run). For ML we eventually want "all applicable rejections", but the first-fail approach matches how humans think about rejections and is sufficient for near-miss detection.
- Covered straddle strategy was not instrumented with rejections (optional, lower priority — only runs if user owns stock + has cash secured)
- Open positions from before the 2026-04-15 hotfix have `conviction=NULL` on the trade row and will fall through to the 0.70 default ladder in `compute_trailing_stop_long`. Only new trades get conviction-scaled trail relief. Backfill is not worth it — the affected trades cycle out within a few days.
